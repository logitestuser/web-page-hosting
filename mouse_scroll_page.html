Introduction

Logitech International S.A. is a Swiss-American multinational manufacturer of computer peripherals and software, with headquarters in Lausanne, Switzerland, and Newark, California. The company develops and markets personal peripherals for PC navigation, video communication and collaboration, music and smart homes. Logitech's products are distributed in almost every country around the world through retail channels or via their strategic partnerships with other companies. Products

Logitech is known for its variety of computer peripherals and devices:

  • Computer Mice: One of Logitech's most well-known product lines, offering everything from basic wired mice to advanced gaming mice.
  • Keyboards: Logitech offers a wide range of keyboards, including mechanical, membrane, and ergonomic designs.
  • Webcams: Logitech webcams are popular for personal and professional use, providing high-quality video for conferencing and streaming.
  • Headsets: Logitech manufactures headsets for gaming, office, and personal use, often featuring noise-cancelling technology.
  • Speakers: The company offers speakers for computers, home audio systems, and portable Bluetooth speakers.
  • Gaming: Logitech G is a brand dedicated to gaming, providing keyboards, mice, headsets, and other accessories designed for gamers.
  • Smart Home Devices: Logitech also produces devices for smart homes, including the Harmony universal remote controls.

Revenue

Logitech has consistently reported strong financial results. For the fiscal year 2021, the company reported:

  • Total Revenue: $5.25 billion, a 76% increase from the previous year.
  • Operating Income: $1.15 billion, up 199% from the previous year.
  • Net Income: $947 million, a significant rise from the previous fiscal year.

The increase in revenue and income was driven by the surge in demand for computer peripherals due to the global shift towards remote work and online activities during the COVID-19 pandemic.
